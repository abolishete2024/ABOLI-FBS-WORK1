Moon - Earth's Natural Satellite

The Moon is Earth's only natural satellite and the fifth largest in the solar system. It influences ocean tides, experiences phases, and has a huge impact on Earth’s natural rhythms.

Scientific Facts

• Diameter: 3,474 km
• Distance from Earth: 384,400 km
• Gravity: 1.62 m/s2
• Temperature: -173°C to 127°C
• No atmosphere, many craters

Moon Phases

The Moon’s appearance changes over ~29 days. These changes are called phases.

Phases: New Moon, Crescent, Half, Gibbous, Full Moon

Tides and Gravity

The Moon’s gravity causes tides in Earth’s oceans. The strongest tides occur during full and new moons (called spring tides).

Moon Exploration

On July 20, 1969, Neil Armstrong became the first person to walk on the Moon during NASA’s Apollo 11 mission.

Astronaut on the Moon during Apollo mission

Moon’s Surface

The surface of the Moon has many craters, mountains, and flat plains called maria. It appears grey and dusty.
