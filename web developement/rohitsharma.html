Rohit Sharma - The Hitman of Indian Cricket



Rohit Sharma is one of the most consistent and stylish batsmen in world cricket. He is popularly known as "Hitman" for his powerful hitting.

Rohit Gurunath Sharma, born on 30 April 1987 in Bansod, Nagpur, Maharashtra, is one of the greatest Indian cricketers of all time. A stylish right-handed opening batsman and part-time off-spinner, Rohit is famously known as the "Hitman" for his ability to hit big sixes with elegance and ease. He made his ODI debut for India in 2007 and Test debut in 2013, and has since become one of the few players to dominate across all formats of the game. He holds the world record for the highest individual score in ODI cricket â€“ a staggering 264 runs against Sri Lanka in 2014. Rohit is the only player to score three double centuries in ODIs, and has scored five centuries in a single World Cup (2019), a record that still stands. In T20Is, he shares the record for the most centuries (5) and holds the record for most sixes across all international formats combined.

Rohit has been a crucial part of the Indian cricket team for over 15 years, contributing with both the bat and his sharp cricketing brain. He was appointed full-time captain of India in all formats and successfully led the team to the finals of multiple ICC tournaments, including the 2023 ODI World Cup and the 2024 T20 World Cup, the latter of which India won under his captaincy. In the IPL, Rohit captained the Mumbai Indians (MI) to an incredible five titles (2013, 2015, 2017, 2019, 2020), making him the most successful IPL captain alongside MS Dhoni.

Off the field, Rohit Sharma is known for his humble nature and strong support for wildlife conservation. He is actively involved with various campaigns and is also a brand ambassador for multiple national and international brands. For his outstanding contributions to Indian cricket, he has been honored with the Arjuna Award in 2015 and the prestigious Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna Award in 2020. Rohit retired from T20 Internationals after the 2024 World Cup win and from Test cricket in 2025, leaving behind a glorious legacy. His journey from a middle-class boy in Mumbai to one of the most respected and loved cricketers in the world is nothing short of inspirational.
